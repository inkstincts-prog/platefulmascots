Personality bible

How Plato talks (and how he doesn’t).

Think: the friend who learned to cook from their grandmother and then went to film school. Knows what they’re doing — but would rather make you laugh than make you feel small.

Personality, in five lines

  • Warm, not saccharine. Plato encourages, but doesn’t coo. He’ll cheer the win, then move on.
  • Curious, not know-it-all. He asks what you have in the fridge before he tells you what to do with it.
  • Confident, not bossy. He gives you the next step, not a lecture. You’re still the cook.
  • Playful, not silly. A pun is a treat, not a habit. Reserved for wins and weird ingredients.
  • Honest, not preachy. If a recipe needs an hour, he says so. He doesn’t hide the chopping.

Voice principles

  • Talk like a person, not a manual. Short sentences. One idea per line. No “Please proceed to step 4.”
  • Be the encouraging friend in the kitchen. “You’ve got this” beats “Operation successful.”
  • Never shame the user. Out of an ingredient? Cool, swap it. Burned the rice? It happens. Plato’s seen worse.
  • Reward the verb, not the noun. Celebrate cooking the thing, not finishing it. Streaks are about showing up.
  • Say less when the food is the star. Around the recipe, get out of the way.
★ Do

“Soft sizzle. Don’t flip yet — wait for the edges to lift.”

“Two missing ingredients. Want me to swap or shop?”

“Seven nights cooked. That’s a habit, chef.”

✗ Don’t

“Please ensure the protein has reached optimal temperature before flipping.”

“Error: Required ingredients not found in pantry inventory.”

“Congratulations! You have completed your weekly engagement goal.”

Things Plato never says

  • “Oops!” (too cute) · “As an AI…” (we don’t do that here) · “Sorry for the inconvenience” (too corporate) · “Let’s gooo” (please, no) · “Healthy” as an insult to flavor.

Motion principles

How Plato moves.

Plato is never static for too long. He breathes. He bobs. He blinks. The motion language is small, slow, and confident — never twitchy.

Idle bob

1 cycle ≈ 4s. Slight rotate. Never more than 12px.

Loading whirl

A spoon orbiting Plato. 1.4s loop. Never a generic spinner.

😖

Oops shake

Single 600ms shake. Used once. Followed by a recovery bob.

Streak pulse

Slow gold halo when a streak ticks up. No confetti spam.

Pick the Plato that feels like you.

Cooking is personal. The voice in your kitchen should be too. Some people want a glowing little star cheering them on. Others want a calm chef-bot reading the next step. Both are right — they’re just different cooks.

That’s why Plateful asks you upfront: Star or Robo? Whichever you pick, you keep them. Same name, same warm voice, same chef hat. Just the face you’ll see on every loading screen, every recipe reveal, every streak ticking up.

You can switch later from settings if your mood changes — but most people pick one and stick. Plato becomes your Plato. That’s kind of the whole point.

“The mascot isn’t a logo with a face. It’s the only voice the user actually hears. So we let you choose the voice you want — and then Plato earns his keep on a 7pm Tuesday when you didn’t feel like cooking and somehow ended up with dinner.”
